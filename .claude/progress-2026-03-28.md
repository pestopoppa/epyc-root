## Field Report: Nemotron-Cascade 2 vs Qwen3.5-35B-A3B (intake-233, intake-234)

**Critical architecture comparison** from colleague GPU benchmark on RTX 3090:

| Model | Architecture | Speed | Context | Flags |
|-------|-------------|-------|---------|-------|
| Nemotron-Cascade 2 (30B-A3B) | Mamba2 hybrid MoE | **187 t/s** | 4K-625K flat | `-ngl 99 -np 1` |
| Qwen3.5-35B-A3B | Delta Net hybrid MoE | **112 t/s** | 4K-262K flat | `-ngl 99 -np 1 -c 262144 --cache-type-k q8_0 --cache-type-v q8_0` |

- **67% speed advantage** for Mamba2 at same active parameter count
- Nemotron needs **no KV cache flags** (auto-allocates 625K), Qwen needs q8_0 KV quant for 262K
- Both architectures show **flat generation speed** (context-independent) — zero degradation at long context
- Nemotron quality: IMO gold (35pts), AIME 92.4, IOI gold, ICPC gold — **significantly exceeds** our 35B-A3B
- Key question: **does llama.cpp support Mamba2 MoE?** If GPU-only, not directly usable in our CPU stack

This finding has implications for:
- **SSM hybrid acceleration**: Mamba2 may be superior SSM variant for inference speed
- **Dynamic stack assembly**: Nemotron-Cascade 2 as potential worker/coder replacement if CPU support lands
- **Architecture evaluation**: Delta Net vs Mamba2 head-to-head data we didn't have before
