## Episodic embedding repair and memory-residency guard

### Trigger

The operator asked to use the stopped-AutoPilot window to investigate episodic FAISS coverage (`31.9%`, ~118k orphan routing memories) and day-over-day RAM drift. They authorized rebuilding embeddings as needed and asked whether AutoPilot should periodically check/reclaim retained memory.

### Findings

- `episodic.db` was current to 2026-06-03, but `reembedded.npz`, `embeddings.faiss`, and `id_map.npy` were all last written on 2026-05-28. Routing memories continued accumulating after the index stopped being maintained.
- The permanent embedding servers were present on 8090-8095, but progressive FAISS persistence was not multiprocess-safe: multiple long-lived `EpisodicStore` instances could assign from stale local FAISS `ntotal` state, producing duplicate `embedding_idx` values and stale durable artifacts.
- RAM drift was dominated by `llama-server` resident memory, especially private dirty KV/context arenas plus mlocked model pages. It was not an ordinary orchestrator/API Python heap leak. `drop_caches` is not the right remedy for mlocked/private-dirty llama memory.
- The permanent embedders were initially running at `nice=+5`, which made them lose scheduling under bulk rebuild pressure and hit 120s request timeouts. Reloading `embedders` brought them back at `nice=0`; this appears to have been stale runtime state, not current launcher behavior.
