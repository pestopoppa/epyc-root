## AutoPilot crash cleanup, dashboard consistency, and orchestrator reload

### Trigger

The operator reported that AutoPilot crashed immediately after launch, left orphan inference traffic visible in the dashboard, and later halted itself after five consecutive metric-free `distill_knowledge` actions. The operator also requested cleanup of polluted trials 187/188/189/190, a rewind so the next launch started at trial 187, an AutoPilot restart, dashboard investigation, and finally an orchestrator reload.

### Root cause / findings

- The planner could repeatedly choose metric-free meta actions after a first allowed bookkeeping turn. Existing `MAX_CONSECUTIVE_META=5` eventually halted AutoPilot, but it did not force the loop back into measured experimentation before the halt.
- Dashboard backend endpoints were mostly correct during the incident: `/dashboard/api/process_status` and `/dashboard/api/autopilot_progress` showed the process/trial state, and the planner/autopilot SSE endpoints emitted content when curled.
- The visible dashboard could still show stale or blank state because the page/API fetches were cacheable, `process_status` and `autopilot_progress` could briefly disagree, region-lock refresh errors were swallowed, and planner/autopilot log panels relied solely on EventSource with no visible fallback/status.
- The trial cleanup was a runtime-state repair, not a code-only fix. Live journal/state/memory artifacts had to be rewound before the restart.
