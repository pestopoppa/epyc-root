### Deep-Dive Findings

Performed deep-dive analysis on both entries. Key findings:

**intake-408 (Tulving Benchmark)**:
- Scoring is 95% deterministic (string matching) — integrates cleanly with our F1 infrastructure
- Reasoning models catastrophically fail at 100K tokens: o1 drops -61% recall, -95% chronological. DeepSeek-R1 drops -42%/-85%. Only Gemini-2.5 survives (-1%/-16%).
- RAG chunk granularity critical: paragraph-level RAG *degrades* recall vs in-context. Event-boundary-aligned chunking required.
- Pre-generated datasets on Figshare — no generation pipeline needed for integration.

**intake-409 (EM-LLM)**:
- Full integration NOT viable: requires per-layer KV access, unified softmax, attention kernel mods in llama.cpp. 4-8 weeks C++ work.
- RAM blocker: 10M tokens = 1.2TB FP16 KV cache. 1M tokens = 125GB.
- YaRN remains better ROI for our stack.
- Salvageable: surprisal-based chunking (logprob spike → chunk boundary) for ColBERT pipeline. Days of work, partial benefit.
