### intake-276 — Brevity Constraints

Our "be concise" prompts are the **weakest form** of brevity constraint tested. Key novel findings:
1. **Explicit word limits outperform**: "under 50 words" >> "be concise" (TALE: +3.1pp GSM8K, CCoT: 30-60 word sweet spot)
2. **Model-tier differentiation needed**: Large models (>=32B) get 60% reduction, small barely affected
3. **Inverse scaling is domain-specific**: BoolQ 11.3%, MMLU-STEM 3.9% — comprehension > math
4. **Verbosity signals uncertainty** (ACL 2025): long after conciseness = uncertain = escalate
5. **Raw length negatively correlates with accuracy** (r=-0.544): our 1.5x alarm may be too conservative
6. **OckBench**: Small models may be MORE expensive per correct answer due to overthinking

### Model Profile

- **Architecture**: 744B total / 40B active, 256 experts (8 active/token), 80 layers, GLM-MoE-DSA
- **Key techniques**: Dynamic Sparse Attention (DSA), Multi-Token Prediction (MTP), Multi-Latent Attention (MLA)
- **Training**: 28.5T tokens, progressive context extension (32K→128K→200K), 160B unique SWE tokens
- **License**: MIT (fully open weights)
- **GGUF sizes**: Q4_K_M=456GB, Q3_K_M=360GB, Q2_K=276GB, UD-IQ2_XXS=241GB

### Deep-Dive Findings — Three Blockers for Local Deployment

**Blocker 1 — Size**: Q4_K_M at 456GB exceeds our ~250GB working memory budget. REAP-50% variant (0xSero/GLM-5-REAP-50pct-FP8, 381B params) would be ~230GB at Q4 — borderline, but no GGUF conversion available.

**Blocker 2 — llama.cpp DSA not implemented**: PR#19460 (merged 2026-02-13) adds GlmMoeDsa arch but DSA indexer tensors are unused. Model runs dense O(L²) attention. Measured: 11-13 tok/s gen, PPL 8.75 at Q4. Follow-up indexer PR discussed but not submitted.

**Blocker 3 — REAP quality unknown**: 0xSero's REAP-50% variant has zero published benchmarks. Quality impact of 50% expert pruning on coding/agentic tasks is entirely unknown.

### Technique Relevance Corrections

- **DSA ≠ KV-cache optimization**: Original assessment was wrong. DSA selects WHICH tokens to attend to (sparse token selection). Our Hadamard work compresses HOW KV values are stored. Different problem layers. Reclassified DSA as context_extension technique.
- **MTP results don't transfer**: Our completed/mtp-speculative-decoding.md found MTP NOT VIABLE on hybrid (0.56x). GLM-5 is pure MoE — different architecture class. Their MTP works because draft and target share the MoE backbone.
- **Cross-stage distillation**: The progressive RL pipeline's cross-stage distillation (recovers skills lost in later RL stages) is conceptually interesting for future SFT work but not immediately actionable.

### Recommended Actions (Amended)

1. ~~Monitor REAP on GLM-5~~ → **REAP-50% exists but blocked** (no GGUF, no benchmarks, no DSA). Monitor, don't act.
2. ~~DSA technique review for KV-cache~~ → **Reclassified**. DSA is context_extension, not kv_cache. Not relevant to current workloads (4K-32K context).
3. **Do NOT deploy locally** — CONFIRMED. Three independent blockers. All GGUF variants exceed memory budget or require missing DSA.
4. **NEW: Monitor llama.cpp DSA indexer PR** — single highest-leverage event that could unlock GLM-5 family. If merged, REAP-50% Q4 (~230GB) becomes worth benchmarking.
