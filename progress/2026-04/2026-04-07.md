### intake-275 — PufferLib / RL Routing

PufferLib itself is overkill for our routing problem (its value is fast env simulation; our routing env is trivial). But deep-dive uncovered **lightweight RL routing alternatives** directly applicable to EPYC:
- **BaRP** (2510.07429): Lightweight policy network + bandit feedback, 16.8% better at 50% less cost
- **PROTEUS** (2604.00136): Lagrangian RL with cost constraints
- **LLM Bandit** (2502.02743): Online bandit transferring across unseen models

Concrete path: Train tiny MLP (~5-10k params) via PPO on Package A seeding data. Replace heuristic routing rules. Sub-microsecond inference, deploy as 50 lines of C.

### intake-276 — Brevity Constraints

Our "be concise" prompts are the **weakest form** of brevity constraint tested. Key novel findings:
1. **Explicit word limits outperform**: "under 50 words" >> "be concise" (TALE: +3.1pp GSM8K, CCoT: 30-60 word sweet spot)
2. **Model-tier differentiation needed**: Large models (>=32B) get 60% reduction, small barely affected
3. **Inverse scaling is domain-specific**: BoolQ 11.3%, MMLU-STEM 3.9% — comprehension > math
4. **Verbosity signals uncertainty** (ACL 2025): long after conciseness = uncertain = escalate
5. **Raw length negatively correlates with accuracy** (r=-0.544): our 1.5x alarm may be too conservative
6. **OckBench**: Small models may be MORE expensive per correct answer due to overthinking

Added Actions 12-15 to reasoning-compression handoff.
