## Session 1 — Autopilot Seeder Refactor: 3-Way → Dynamic Per-Role Evaluation

### Problem
Autopilot quality was frozen at q=1.138 for ~180 trials (since trial 1 of AR-3 Run 3, 2026-04-12). Investigation revealed three compounding issues:

1. **Eval tower routing collapse**: `eval_tower._eval_question()` sends `force_role=""` for end-to-end quality measurement. This goes through HybridRouter → learned routing (96% uniform Q-values, skipped) → rule-based fallback → `return ["frontdoor"]`. Every sentinel question routed to frontdoor, producing identical 11/29 correct = q=1.138 on every trial.

2. **3-way seeder too abstract**: The seeder's `evaluate_question_3way()` tested 3 hardcoded paths (SELF:direct, SELF:repl, ARCHITECT) instead of individual models. Q-values were keyed by abstract actions ("SELF:direct") not role names ("frontdoor", "architect_general"), so routing never learned per-model preferences. This was a pre-autopilot simplification that became stale.

3. **Blacklist poisoning**: 6 blacklist entries seeded from program.md included 4 server-launch-time flags not tunable at runtime (tree_speculation, dflash, hybrid_speculation), 1 model not in the active registry (nemotron_mamba2), and 1 entry (tool_permissions_legacy) potentially poisoned by recent REPL/tool routing bug fixes.

### Root Cause
The 3-way eval was designed before autopilot existed, as a simplification of the original all-roles `evaluate_question()`. It collapsed per-model signal into 3 coarse categories, preventing Q-values from learning which role handles which question type best. Combined with the eval tower's end-to-end measurement (correct by design, but useless when routing can't learn), autopilot mutations to prompts on architect_general.md, coder_escalation.md, etc. were invisible because no questions ever reached those models.

### Root Cause (infrastructure issues)
Previous NUMA sweep scripts had three critical bugs causing hours of wasted time:
1. **Missing `--mlock`**: Servers used mmap without locking pages, causing eviction/thrashing under concurrent load (0 tps on multi-instance)

### Key Findings

1. **Q8 > Q4 on bandwidth-rich hardware**: For models that fit in RAM, Q8_0's trivial dequant (single multiply) beats Q4_K_M's complex k-quant unpacking. This holds for Qwen3.6 (25.6 vs 24.4) and likely all sub-100GB models on EPYC 9655.

2. **NUMA multi-instance requires mlock + membind**: `taskset` alone is insufficient. Without `numactl --membind=N`, mlock'd pages land on the wrong NUMA node. Without `--mlock`, kernel shares/evicts mmap'd pages. Both cause catastrophic performance (0-0.7 tps instead of expected 10-25 tps).

3. **Concurrent benchmark reveals real scaling**: Previous serial benchmarks showed 4×48t at ~95 tps aggregate (sum of sequential per-instance). Concurrent benchmarks show 4×48t at ~57 tps (actual parallel throughput). The ~40% gap is memory controller contention under real load.

4. **M2.7 architect replacement viable**: 11.1 tps (Q8) consolidates both architect_general (9.14) and architect_coding (3.79) into one model. Quality eval (G9) is the remaining gate.

## Session 4 — Research Intake: Target Policy Optimization (TPO)

### Problem

### Analysis
Investigated the autopilot's full architecture to evaluate feasibility:

- **Autopilot control surface**: 23 numeric params (5 surfaces), 43 binary feature flags, open-ended prompt/code mutations — mixed discrete+continuous+text action space
- **Current optimizer**: NSGA-II (Optuna) with 4D Pareto scoring (quality, speed, -cost, reliability)
- **Episode structure**: Single-step bandit (observe → act → evaluate), not sequential — no credit assignment across time steps

**Key insight**: TPO's target construction `q_i ∝ p_old * exp(score/η)` + cross-entropy fitting IS the Cross-Entropy Method (CEM). CEM is directly applicable to the 23-param numeric surface as an alternative/augmentation to NSGA-II. Full 66+ dim control surface embedding is infeasible (heterogeneous action space, expensive evaluations make K-rollout groups prohibitive).

**Concrete integration point**: When `pareto_archive.py:hypervolume_slope() < 0.001` triggers stagnation detection, switch from NSGA-II to CEM sampling as the exploration boost mechanism.
