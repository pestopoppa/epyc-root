## Session 11 — Autopilot trial-progress bar: tick-style distribution rendering

### Trigger

Operator: *"Autopilot's dashboard has a trial progress bar that always overruns thus serving no informative purpose whatsoever. See example here: 'trial #559 (rollback) 99% (overrun) / elapsed 27.3m · p50(rollback, n=18) ~5.3m'"*

This is a follow-up to Session 1's fix (which moved the bar from a hardcoded 1200s fallback to journal-derived p50). The earlier fix made the number correct in principle but exposed a deeper design flaw: **using p50 as the bar denominator means ~50% of trials saturate it by definition**, and for heavy-tailed actions like `rollback` the saturation rate is higher.

### Root cause

`/dashboard/api/autopilot_progress` set `expected_s = recent_p50` then computed `percent = min(99, elapsed / p50 * 100)`. Live numbers for trial #559 at the moment of capture:

| field | value |
|---|---|
| elapsed | 1814 s (30.2 min) |
| p50(rollback, n=18) | 321 s (5.4 min) |
| p90(rollback) | **1930 s (32.2 min)** — 6× p50 |
| avg(rollback) | 606 s (2× p50) |

Elapsed at p87 of historical distribution → late but not unprecedented. The bar can't communicate that because it's pinned at 99% from ~5 min onward.

### Design choice

Operator picked **option 3: tick-style distribution bar** (bar fills to p90, ticks at p25/p50/p75, color shifts past p75/p90).

### Changes (epyc-orchestrator only)
