## Per-model throughput audit (27B / 31B / 122B-A10B)

User asked why Qwen3.6-27B Q4_K_M, SuperGemma4-31B Q4_K_M, and Qwen3.5-122B-A10B Q4_K_M run at single-digit t/s on EPYC despite ~460 GB/s aggregate BW. Mental model under audit was "BW = 460 GB/s, so dense 27-31B should easily hit double-digit decode."

### Finding: the gap is mostly physics + arch, not config

Pulled v5 canonical numbers from `epyc-inference-research/benchmarks/results/runs/20260430_151713/`:

| Model | Quant | Weights | Theoretical (peak BW) | Realistic (24% util ≈ 110 GB/s) | v5 measured | Verdict |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Qwen3.6-27B (dense hybrid GDN+Attn) | Q4_K_M | 16.3 GB | ~28 t/s | ~7.4 t/s | **6.65 t/s** | at ceiling |
| Qwen3.6-27B | Q8_0 | 27.0 GB | ~17 t/s | ~4.1 t/s | **4.54 t/s** | at ceiling |
| SuperGemma4-31B (dense) | Q4_K_M | 17.4 GB | ~26 t/s | ~6.3 t/s | **6.87 t/s** | at ceiling |
| Qwen3.5-122B-A10B (MoE) | Q4_K_M | 69 GB / ~10B active | varies | varies | **4.3 t/s/instance** | unmeasured under canonical (probe queued) |

The 460 GB/s ceiling assumes 100% BW utilization. Per `qwen36-27b-cpu-feasibility.md:44` and `cpu-shape-specialized-gemv-decode.md:165`, dense decode on EPYC 9655 actually realizes ~24% (110 GB/s effective) due to barrier overhead × hybrid op count + scalar fallback paths + KV traffic. perf-stat shows 0.17 IPC and 96.6% backend-stalled-on-memory at 96 threads — decisive DRAM-bound confirmation.

For the 27B/31B at single-digit t/s the answer is: **at canonical ceiling, not a config bug**. For 122B-A10B (4.3 t/s) the v5 deployment draft has the role under `todo_or_undecided` (line 214) — never had a clean per-arch canonical measurement. Plausible 2× single-instance headroom, queued for Probe B.

Initial framing of "OMP env stack + GGML_NUMA_WEIGHTS not in production = 3-4× hit" was corrected mid-session: those levers compound on **MoE** workloads, but per the v5 deployment matrix `dense_q8` and `dense_q4` rows explicitly mark all CPU1/mbind-off probes as **net-negative** for the 27B/31B class.

### Deep-dive of intake-521 — six material corrections (research/deep-dives/qwen-scope-sae-suite.md)

User asked to deep-dive the intake. Skeptical re-read surfaced six issues that materially narrowed the practical adoption pathway:

1. **License is `qwen` custom, not Apache 2.0.** Confirmed via HF model-card metadata. Section 9.3 of the paper additionally prohibits using SAEs to "interfere with model capabilities" with author-reserved final interpretation. Section 4 / 5 post-hoc analysis is unambiguously permitted; Section 7 / 8 behavior-modification adoption requires a license-clause review.
2. **Storage cost is ≈ 687 GB FP32 / 344 GB FP16** for the production-stack subset (both L0 settings each for Qwen3-1.7B + Qwen3.5-27B + Qwen3-30B-A3B + Qwen3.5-35B-A3B). Pull per-application, not en bloc; SAE-Res-Qwen3.5-27B-W80K-L0_50 alone is ~213 GB.
3. **Wang et al. 2026 (ICLR 2026, OpenReview Q4ooLNOFeR) is cited in Qwen-Scope's own reference list but not benchmarked against.** Reports Kendall tau-b ~0.298 SAE-interpretability vs steering-utility correlation across Gemma-2-2B + **Qwen-2.5-3B (0.458)** + Gemma-2-9B; falls to ~0 or negative under Delta Token Confidence-based feature selection.
4. **AxBench (Wu et al. ICML 2025, OpenReview K2CckZjNy0, Stanford NLP) is NOT cited by Qwen-Scope.** Prompting and finetuning beat SAE steering on Gemma-2; difference-in-means dominates SAEs at concept detection — exactly the regime of Qwen-Scope Section 5's toxicity classifier. Qwen-Scope does not run any of these baselines.
5. **Section 5 toxicity-classifier baselines are absent.** F1 0.92/0.96 reported with no comparison against (a) linear probe on the same residual stream, (b) difference-in-means, (c) trained classification head, (d) BERT/RoBERTa-class toxicity models. Performance claim is uncontextualized; SAE classifier should be framed as audit primitive, not primary classifier.
6. **Section 8 repetition-feature methodology has a structural confound.** Features are selected by within-sample first-vs-last-occurrence comparison of the same token, mechanically picking "rises during repetition." Same features fire on benign repetition (Figure 21). The +5.84pp MGSM Qwen3-30B-A3B RL+SAE result cannot be cleanly attributed to repetition suppression without an OOD-vs-targeted ablation.

Plus one related finding: **SASFT (Section 7) is a separately-published ICLR 2026 paper** by lead author Boyi Deng (OpenReview BQOFU9qO5j) — peer-reviewed status higher than the rest of the technical report, but Section 7 Table 5 itself shows Qwen3-8B HellaSwag -2.88 / MMLU -2.06 regressions consistent with Wang 2026's utility falsification.
