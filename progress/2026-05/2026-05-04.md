## Per-model throughput audit (27B / 31B / 122B-A10B)

User asked why Qwen3.6-27B Q4_K_M, SuperGemma4-31B Q4_K_M, and Qwen3.5-122B-A10B Q4_K_M run at single-digit t/s on EPYC despite ~460 GB/s aggregate BW. Mental model under audit was "BW = 460 GB/s, so dense 27-31B should easily hit double-digit decode."

### Finding: the gap is mostly physics + arch, not config

Pulled v5 canonical numbers from `epyc-inference-research/benchmarks/results/runs/20260430_151713/`:

| Model | Quant | Weights | Theoretical (peak BW) | Realistic (24% util ≈ 110 GB/s) | v5 measured | Verdict |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Qwen3.6-27B (dense hybrid GDN+Attn) | Q4_K_M | 16.3 GB | ~28 t/s | ~7.4 t/s | **6.65 t/s** | at ceiling |
| Qwen3.6-27B | Q8_0 | 27.0 GB | ~17 t/s | ~4.1 t/s | **4.54 t/s** | at ceiling |
| SuperGemma4-31B (dense) | Q4_K_M | 17.4 GB | ~26 t/s | ~6.3 t/s | **6.87 t/s** | at ceiling |
| Qwen3.5-122B-A10B (MoE) | Q4_K_M | 69 GB / ~10B active | varies | varies | **4.3 t/s/instance** | unmeasured under canonical (probe queued) |

The 460 GB/s ceiling assumes 100% BW utilization. Per `qwen36-27b-cpu-feasibility.md:44` and `cpu-shape-specialized-gemv-decode.md:165`, dense decode on EPYC 9655 actually realizes ~24% (110 GB/s effective) due to barrier overhead × hybrid op count + scalar fallback paths + KV traffic. perf-stat shows 0.17 IPC and 96.6% backend-stalled-on-memory at 96 threads — decisive DRAM-bound confirmation.

For the 27B/31B at single-digit t/s the answer is: **at canonical ceiling, not a config bug**. For 122B-A10B (4.3 t/s) the v5 deployment draft has the role under `todo_or_undecided` (line 214) — never had a clean per-arch canonical measurement. Plausible 2× single-instance headroom, queued for Probe B.

Initial framing of "OMP env stack + GGML_NUMA_WEIGHTS not in production = 3-4× hit" was corrected mid-session: those levers compound on **MoE** workloads, but per the v5 deployment matrix `dense_q8` and `dense_q4` rows explicitly mark all CPU1/mbind-off probes as **net-negative** for the 27B/31B class.
