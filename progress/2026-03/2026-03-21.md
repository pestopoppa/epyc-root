### Key Findings

1. **NUMA helps MoE/small models, hurts dense models:**
   - 30B-A3B (+36%), 122B (+75%), worker 7B (+20%) — all benefit from NUMA pinning
   - Dense 32B coders: -11% to -19% — bandwidth-bound, fewer threads hurts

2. **Previously assumed optimal params were mostly wrong:**
   - 30B-A3B: dm=32→**8** (dm irrelevant at 48t, all values 38-39 t/s)
   - Q4KM: dm=24/ps=0→**dm=32/ps=0.05** (tree helps, overturns "tree negative on Q4KM")
   - Q8: dm=24→**dm=16** at 48t; tree helps at 192t (ps=0.3) but hurts at 48t
   - 480B: dm=48/ps=0.05→**dm=24/ps=0** (tree is -19% harmful, not +9% beneficial)
   - 122B: dm=8→**dm=24** (but dm is nearly flat, spec barely helps hybrid arch)
   - f16: dm=32/ps=0.05→unchanged (correct!)

3. **Tree speculation is model-dependent, not quant-dependent:**
   - Q4KM: ps=0.05 wins (overturns prior assumption)
   - Q8: mode-dependent (192t: ps=0.3, 48t: ps=0)
   - 480B + 122B: tree devastating (-19% to -40%)

4. **Registry throughput values were inflated:**
   - coder Q4KM: 39.4→10.8 (3.6x inflated — from single warm-cache summarization prompt)
   - worker 7B: 43.9→19.2 (2.3x inflated — 96t with -np 2 and 50-token generation)
   - Q-scorer in orchestrator using these inflated baselines — biasing routing

5. **Worker 7B f16 outclassed by freed 30B-A3B:**
   - 30B-A3B at 48t: 39.1 t/s vs 7B at 24t: 19.2 t/s (2x faster, better quality, similar RAM)
