# 2026-03-24 Progress

## Coder Quant Quality Benchmark — Complete

Scored 122 responses (61 per model) for Q8_0 and f16 variants of Qwen2.5-Coder-32B using Claude-as-Judge methodology. Scoring calibrated against existing Q4KM baseline (74%).

### Quality Results

| Variant | Raw Score | Pass Rate (≥2) | Speed (48t) | 4×agg | RAM/inst |
|---------|-----------|----------------|-------------|-------|----------|
| **Q4_K_M** | 133/183 (72.7%) | **45/61 (74%)** | 10.8 t/s | ~43.3 | 18.5 GB |
| Q8_0 | 136/183 (74.3%) | 47/61 (77%) | 8.2 t/s | ~32.7 | 33 GB |
| f16 | 133/183 (72.7%) | 45/61 (74%) | 6.4 t/s | ~25.6 | 65 GB |

### Decision: Q4_K_M Confirmed

f16 offers **zero quality improvement** over Q4KM (identical 74% pass rate, identical 133/183 raw score) while being 41% slower and using 3.5x more RAM. Q8 gains only +2 questions at 24% speed cost. The quality ceiling is the model architecture, not quantization — all three quants have identical instruction_precision scores (20/33).

### Key Findings
- Prompt echo: Both Q8 and f16 echo full prompts before responding (likely chat template issue at lower TPS)
- Repetition loops: f16 showed more degeneration than Q4KM on some questions
- Math improvement: Q8 solved horse proof and probability theory correctly (Q4KM/f16 failed both)
- RAM savings: Switching from f16 to Q4KM saves **186 GB** across 4 NUMA instances (260->74 GB)
