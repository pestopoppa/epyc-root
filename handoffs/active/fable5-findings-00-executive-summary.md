## The one-paragraph answer

You asked four architecture questions. The evidence says they are one problem wearing four costumes: **your system's binding constraint is decision-grade evidence — the ability to measure an effect, attribute it, prove what was running when it was measured, and keep refuted stories dead.** The autopilot mines noise because its instrument resolves ~2 question-flips while its actions produce ~0–2 (facet 1+2); routing "accretion" is mostly dark code plus a learner starved to 96%-uniform Q-values, with the live system already simple (facet 3); the serving layer leaves measured config wins unharvested while the GPU's highest-compounding use — becoming the *measurement engine* — isn't in your hypothesis (facet 4); and the backlog/indices have become narrative stores exhibiting the same fact/policy/narrative conflation as the autopilot's memory (deliverable 3). The architecture to buy is not a cleverer optimizer or router: it is an **evidence plane** — append-only measurement ledger with per-question outcomes and provenance, sequential paired statistics, running-state attestation, model-capability descriptors, and narrative that is regenerated rather than replayed. Every piece has a cheap first step, and several pieces already exist in your codebase waiting to be promoted (e.g. `journal_reconstruction.py` is the event-sourcing engine, used only by the dashboard).

## Verdicts on your six hypotheses (§6 of the brief)

1. *"CPU decode is bandwidth-bound and exhausted; next gains architectural."* — **Confirmed at the kernel level** (your 2026-04-26 canonical re-measurement is decisive). **Refuted at the serving level**: frontdoor runs zero spec-dec (α unmeasured, CPU-testable today), continuous batching unused, 96t operating point unused. You are not yet standing at the wall everywhere you think you are. (findings-03 §1)
2. *"Autopilot contamination is structural, not a bug sequence."* — **Confirmed, strongly.** 12 incidents, fixes scattered across ≥9 files; three distinct planes (metrology / decision policy / narrative) each failing for its own reason; narrative-class incidents all leaked through persisted-narrative stores that write-time fixes cannot clean (incl. the resumed cloud-CLI session — a store outside every scrub). But the deepest structural defect is **statistical, not architectural**: the instrument can't certify the effects being chased. (findings-01 §1–2)
3. *"Routing lost its unifying principle; simpler under one decision model."* — **Reframed.** The unifying principle exists in your code (everything estimates `Q ≈ P(success) − λ·cost`); the live stack is already simple; the accretion is dark mass + missing weights + data starvation. The fix is enforcement and deletion, not new decision theory. Two infrastructure bugs matter more than any redesign: **production runs on test feature defaults**, and **runtime flag changes reach 1 of 6 uvicorn workers** — the latter means historical flag-toggle experiments never fully reached the system under test. (findings-02 §2, verified)
4. *"MI210 = dense frontdoor + GPU draft/MTP heads accelerating CPU targets."* — **Half-confirmed, emphasis inverted.** Frontdoor *residency* is the headline (~3× from bandwidth alone; your own handoff buries its ~85 t/s bound as a detail). GPU-drafting CPU targets is the weakest leg (PCIe round-trips, α-bounded ceiling, your own net-negative spec-dec history); keep its ≥1.3× kill-gate. Missing from your hypothesis: **GPU as eval engine** (statistical power per wall-hour — compounds with facet 1+2), embedder/classifier host (unblocks routing retrain), prefill offload. (findings-03 §2)
5. *"Backlog should be a toolbox the orchestration learns to deploy."* — **Confirmed for the ~22 flag/knob-shaped capabilities, refuted as a general premise.** Today the backlog reaches the planner only as a denylist; the promotion pattern ran once; ~0% of measured speed levers are autopilot-actionable. But don't expose surfaces before the instrument can ratify them — sequence: measurement first, then the capability registry with an `actionable-by` field. (findings-04 §1.3, §3.3)
6. *"We optimize within unquestioned framings."* — **Confirmed. Drop first: "the eval tower is fixed infrastructure."** Then: "single-user latency-first serving" (your dominant workload is the harness itself), then "backlog = TODO list." (findings-04 §1)

## What you are missing (the highest-value contribution, per your §0)

1. **The instrument is the product.** Your traffic is mostly your own evaluator; your optimizer's ceiling is your evaluator's resolution; your operator-trust budget is spent on evaluator failures. Yet the eval tower is the one component declared immutable and given no roadmap. Invert that: the eval/evidence plane deserves the engineering attention routing has been getting.
2. **Attestation before self-healing.** A system that cannot prove what is running (test-defaults bug, 1-of-6 flag divergence, flag-on/weights-missing, stated-vs-running architecture drift) cannot cure itself — it can't even diagnose itself. Cheapest invariant on the list.
3. **Per-question outcomes are free power.** The fixed 43-question set is a repeated-measures design; you discard the pairing at aggregation. Persisting 43 bits/trial converts "never certifiable" 1–2-flip effects into 1–2-trial McNemar detections. Single highest-leverage change in the system.
4. **The descriptor seam.** Months of benchmarking feed a registry the router never reads. A versioned model-capability descriptor is the missing interface that makes model-agnosticism real, makes routing transferable across swaps, and lets acceleration config travel with models.
5. **Live defects to fix this week** (found during review, all verified): T1 baseline ratcheted to noise-max at trial 775 — the −5% gate is now failing ~half of honest trials *as you read this*; double `gate.check()` per mutation trial; dead throughput floor (12.7 vs ~50 t/s) disabling throttle remediation; T0's harder sentinels dead behind a `[:10]` slice; strategy-store write leak on within-noise frontier admissions; distill reads corrupt rows unfiltered. (findings-01 §3) Additionally, the dual-write drift check was executed during this review: **the live T2 Pareto frontier has already desynced from the journal** (live {363} vs reconstructed {363, 367}) — the event-sourcing recommendation is grounded in observed drift, not theory. (findings-01 §4)
