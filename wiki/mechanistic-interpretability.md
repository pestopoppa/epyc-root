# Mechanistic Interpretability

**Category**: `mechanistic_interpretability`
**Confidence**: developing
**Last compiled**: 2026-05-04
**Sources**: 3 intake entries (521, 522, 523), 2 dedicated deep-dives, 1 coordination handoff

## Summary

Mechanistic interpretability is the subfield concerned with reverse-engineering the internal computations of large language models — not just describing what they output, but isolating the specific representations and circuits that produce that behavior. Sparse autoencoders (SAEs) have emerged as the dominant tooling: they decompose a model's residual-stream activation at each layer into a sparse, overcomplete dictionary of features, each interpretable as a "concept direction." For EPYC the practical question is narrower than the academic frame — *can SAEs deliver actionable signal for routing, evaluation, or post-training without paying more in compute / storage / quality regressions than they save?* As of 2026-05-04 the answer is qualified: post-hoc analysis (benchmark-suite redundancy, prompt fingerprinting, classifier audit traces) is genuinely useful; behavior modification (steering, training-time feature suppression) is not yet convincingly better than simpler baselines.

The release that put EPYC adjacency on the map is **Qwen-Scope** (Qwen Team, 2026-04-30), an open-source SAE suite covering 14 SAE groups across 7 Qwen3 / Qwen3.5 backbones — including Qwen3-1.7B (drafter), Qwen3.5-27B-Instruct (frontdoor candidate), Qwen3-30B-A3B (production worker), and Qwen3.5-35B-A3B (qwen36-upgrade predecessor). The suite uses TopK SAEs at the residual stream of every layer, expansion factors 16x for dense and up to 64x for MoE, with widths 32K - 128K. Storage is non-trivial: ~687 GB FP32 for the EPYC-relevant subset both L0 settings; per-application pulls (one model, one or two middle layers, ~3-7 GB) are the practical posture. License is `qwen` custom (not Apache 2.0); paper Section 9.3 explicitly prohibits using the SAEs to "interfere with model capabilities," which creates operational ambiguity for steering / SASFT / SAE-DAPO adoption but does not affect post-hoc analysis use.

The skeptical baseline is anchored by two complementary results that Qwen-Scope cites (Wang et al. 2026) or that pre-date it (Wu et al. 2025 / AxBench). **AxBench** (Stanford NLP, ICML 2025) benchmarks SAE-based methods against prompting, finetuning, difference-in-means, linear probes, and a novel ReFT-r1 method on Gemma-2-2B-it / -9B-it: prompting beats every method on steering, finetuning is second, and difference-in-means dominates SAEs on concept detection — the regime that includes Qwen-Scope's Section 5 toxicity classifier. **Wang et al. 2026** (ICLR 2026) goes further by training 90 SAEs across 5 architectures × 6 sparsity levels on Gemma-2-2B / Qwen-2.5-3B / Gemma-2-9B and measuring per-SAE Kendall tau between SAEBench auto-interpretability score and AxBench steering harmonic mean: τb ≈ 0.298 overall (Qwen-2.5-3B specifically: 0.458), falling to ~0 or negative under their Delta Token Confidence feature selector. The mechanism: standard contrastive / activation-frequency / output-score feature selection does not isolate the steering-causal features. Two operational gates fall out as adoptable patterns regardless of which SAE artifact one reaches for: mandatory difference-in-means + linear-probe + prompting baselines for any SAE-feature classifier or steering proposal, and Delta-Token-Confidence-based feature selection (or successor) before crediting feature interpretability as a proxy for utility.

Independent of the steering / classifier debate, Qwen-Scope's **Section 4** offers a cleaner application: SAE feature footprints as an evaluation-free benchmark redundancy / similarity proxy. The paper reports Spearman ρ ≈ 0.85 between feature-coverage AUC redundancy R-hat(D) and performance-redundancy R(D) across 17 benchmarks evaluated with 26 in-house Qwen pre-training checkpoints; partial Pearson 75.5% after partialling out MMLU as a general-ability confound. The signal is in-distribution to Qwen pretraining, but EPYC uses Qwen models on Qwen-distribution prompts, so the in-distribution caveat is not binding. Asymmetric and min-normalized feature overlap reveal intuitive containment relationships (63% of GSM8K's features are covered by MATH but only 10% the other way; code benchmarks form a tight cluster; broad knowledge benchmarks subsume specialized ones). This is the highest-signal Section to pilot first against the EPYC eval suite — pull SAE-Res-Qwen3.5-27B-W80K-L0_50 single layer (~3.34 GB), encode the eval prompts, compute feature redundancy and inter-benchmark overlap, propose pruned suite with cross-validation against held-out checkpoint panel.

Qwen-Scope's **Section 8** identifies SAE features causally linked to **endless repetition** — a low-frequency reasoning failure mode where pre-activation values rise monotonically across token positions in repetitive contexts and bidirectional steering establishes causal direction. The paper uses this to motivate **SAE-guided DAPO with rare-negative-rollout augmentation**: inject one SAE-steered repetitive output per group of G in DAPO; on Qwen3-30B-A3B the method drops repeat ratio sharply and yields +5.84pp MGSM relative to the pre-RL checkpoint vs vanilla DAPO's +1.08pp. The paper itself documents an important caveat (Figure 21): the same features fire on benign repetition (instruction-echo, multiple-choice answer-choice repetition), which is why Section 8 chooses rare-negative-rollouts over SASFT-style suppression. For EPYC, this is the closest published analogue to the Ring-mini stuck-in-think failure mode — the methodology (contrastive identification of stuck-state features → causal validation via bidirectional steering → rare-negative augmentation in RL) is portable to any Qwen-family stuck-in-think failure once RL infra is online (DGX Spark not yet acquired). Adoption is triple-blocked on RL infra + a documented Qwen-family failure + an OOD-vs-targeted ablation distinguishing feature-targeted from generic-OOD rare-negatives. Section 7 SASFT (auxiliary feature-suppression loss for code-switching) is documented in a separate ICLR 2026 paper by the lead Qwen-Scope contributor (Deng et al. 2026, OpenReview BQOFU9qO5j) and shows 50-100% code-switching reduction on Qwen3-1.7B / Qwen3-8B, but Section 7 Table 5 itself shows non-trivial general-capability regressions (Qwen3-8B HellaSwag -2.88, MMLU -2.06) consistent with Wang 2026's utility-falsification. Without an EPYC code-switching production failure to motivate the work and without diversity-collapse evaluation hooks (intake-441 family / EV-8) in place, SASFT adoption is deferred.

## Key Findings

- **Qwen-Scope releases SAEs for the EPYC production stack subset.** 14 SAE groups across 7 Qwen3/Qwen3.5 backbones; coverage includes Qwen3-1.7B (drafter), Qwen3.5-27B-Instruct (frontdoor candidate), Qwen3-30B-A3B (production worker), Qwen3.5-35B-A3B (qwen36-upgrade predecessor). Qwen3.6 family is NOT covered. Trained on residual-stream activations using Gao-et-al. TopK recipe with 1/32 auxiliary dead-feature loss and L2-norm outlier filtering. [intake-521, research/deep-dives/qwen-scope-sae-suite.md]
- **Storage cost is meaningful — ~687 GB FP32 for the EPYC-relevant subset both L0 settings.** Per-layer SAE size for SAE-Res-Qwen3.5-27B-W80K is ~3.34 GB FP32; full bundle ~213 GB per L0 variant. Recommend per-application pulls, not full-suite mirroring. [research/deep-dives/qwen-scope-sae-suite.md]
- **License is `qwen` custom, not Apache 2.0.** Section 9.3 of the Qwen-Scope paper prohibits using the SAEs to "interfere with model capabilities." Section 4 / 5 post-hoc analysis is unambiguously permitted; Section 7 / 8 behavior-modification adoption requires a license-clause review. [research/deep-dives/qwen-scope-sae-suite.md]
- **AxBench (Wu et al. ICML 2025) reports prompting and finetuning beat SAE steering on Gemma-2; difference-in-means dominates SAEs on concept detection.** Tested on instruction-tuned Gemma-2-2B-it and Gemma-2-9B-it with GemmaScope SAEs and Neuronpedia-derived concept lists. Higher-quality concept labels improve SAE numbers but do not close the gap to ReFT-r1 / DiffMean. [intake-522]
- **Wang et al. 2026 reports SAE interpretability and steering utility correlate at Kendall τb ≈ 0.298 across 90 SAEs on Gemma-2 + Qwen-2.5-3B; falls to ~0 or negative under Delta Token Confidence selection.** Per-model Psi_C: Gemma-2-2B 0.218, Qwen-2.5-3B 0.458, Gemma-2-9B 0.306. DTC selector improves average steering score by +52.52% over the prior best output-score baseline. Cited in Qwen-Scope's own reference list but Qwen-Scope does not benchmark against it. [intake-523]
- **Two methodological gates extracted as adoptable patterns.** G1 (from AxBench): mandatory difference-in-means + linear-probe + prompting baselines for any SAE-feature classifier or steering proposal — if baselines match within 5pp, the SAE buys interpretability only. G2 (from Wang 2026): validate that selected features score in the top decile under Delta Token Confidence before crediting them as steering-causal. [research/deep-dives/sae-utility-falsification-axbench-wang2026.md]
- **Section 4 benchmark-redundancy via feature footprints is the strongest single application for EPYC.** Spearman 0.85 vs performance-redundancy across 17 benchmarks (in-distribution to Qwen pretraining caveat); asymmetric overlap reveals containment structure (63% of GSM8K's features covered by MATH, 10% reverse). Pull SAE-Res-Qwen3.5-27B-W80K-L0_50 single middle-band layer (~3.34 GB), encode EPYC eval prompts, propose pruned suite with held-out cross-validation. [intake-521 Section 4, handoffs/active/eval-tower-verification.md]
- **Section 5 SAE classifier should be framed as audit primitive, not primary classifier.** F1 0.92 (Qwen3-1.7B) / 0.96 (Qwen3-8B) on English toxicity reported with no comparison against difference-in-means (the AxBench-recommended baseline for concept detection), linear probe, or trained head. Useful for transparency atop a learned classifier; not a deployment-grade replacement. [intake-521 Section 5, intake-522, handoffs/active/routing-intelligence.md]
- **Section 8 rare-negative-rollout augmentation in DAPO is the most promising behavior-modification regime — and the most defensible against the steering-degradation literature.** The model learns to *avoid* SAE-steered outputs rather than imitate them, sidestepping the core failure mode AxBench documents. +5.84pp MGSM on Qwen3-30B-A3B vs vanilla DAPO's +1.08pp. Triple-blocked for EPYC: RL infra + documented Qwen-family stuck-in-think failure + OOD-vs-targeted ablation. [intake-521 Section 8, handoffs/active/reasoning-compression.md, handoffs/active/per-request-reasoning-budget.md]
- **Section 8 repetition feature carries a precursor signal for stuck-in-think.** Pre-activation values rise monotonically across token positions before the first repeated token. Could be exposed in llama-server as a per-decode-step scalar alongside the Halo entropy signal (intake-392) — two independent precursor signals for the same internal failure state, both inference-time, neither requires training infra. [intake-521 Section 8, handoffs/active/per-request-reasoning-budget.md]
- **SASFT Section 7 trades targeted reduction for general-capability regression.** Qwen3-8B HellaSwag -2.88pp / MMLU -2.06pp regressions documented in Section 7 Table 5; Wang et al. 2026 utility-falsification on Qwen-2.5-3B aligns with this pattern. Adoption deferred until both RL infra and intake-441 diversity-collapse evaluation hooks land, and a real EPYC code-switching production failure motivates the work. [intake-521 Section 7, intake-523]
- **Inference loading is custom PyTorch.** No transformers / sae_lens integration; no first-party github repo from QwenLM; HF Spaces interactive feature explorer (`Qwen/QwenScope`) is in "Paused" state at deep-dive date. Any EPYC integration writes residual-stream extraction + SAE forward pass + feature-activation tooling itself. Third-party `embeddr-net/ComfyUI-QwenScope` exists but is image-generation-focused. [research/deep-dives/qwen-scope-sae-suite.md]

## Open Questions

- Does the Wang et al. 2026 utility-falsification finding hold when re-run on Qwen-Scope's specific Qwen3 / Qwen3.5 SAEs (rather than the 16K-width SAEs they trained themselves)? Qwen-Scope widths are 32K-128K — wider dictionaries may have different interpretability-utility tradeoffs.
- Does Section 8's repetition-feature methodology survive the Falsifying-SAE-Reasoning-Features framework (Ma et al. 2026, arxiv:2601.05679)? The paper is cited in Qwen-Scope's own reference list but Section 9.2 does not engage with it.
- Are the Qwen3.5-35B-A3B SAEs portable to Qwen3.6-35B-A3B post-training? Predecessor → successor SAE transfer is uncharacterized in the paper for MoE+hybrid-SSM architectures; only Qwen3 vs Qwen3.5 longitudinal comparisons exist.
- What is the actual EPYC CPU cost of running an SAE forward pass on the residual stream at one chosen layer per token? TopK over a 32K-128K dictionary is small relative to the 30B-A3B forward pass, but BW-bound reality on EPYC 9655 needs measurement before any classifier or eval-time use becomes routine.
